The Recording Prompt Worksheet

Answer these seven prompts out loud. Voice memo, not typing. Then hand the transcript to Claude. This is the raw material your sales page gets built from.

How to use this: Read each prompt, hit record on a voice memo app, and just talk. Don't overthink it or write a script. The messier and more natural, the better copy Claude has to work with. Type a few keyword notes in each box if it helps you remember what to say, then transcribe (or paste) your recording into the matching box before you export.
0 of 7 prompts started
01

the offer, plainly

What is it, exactly?

Say the name of the offer, the price, the format, and what someone gets immediately after they buy.
  • What's it called, and what's the price?
  • Is it a course, a template, a call, a bundle?
  • What do they receive the moment they pay: instant access, a link, a booking?
02

the before

Where is she stuck right now?

Describe her current frustration in her own words, not yours. Rant a little.
  • What's she tried already that hasn't worked?
  • What does an average week look like for her right now, because of this problem?
  • What's she telling herself about why it's not working?
03

the after

What changes once she's done this?

Paint the specific, tangible result. Not a vague feeling.
  • What can she do in 30 days that she can't do today?
  • What does that mean for her business, her time, or her income?
  • Is there a number, timeframe, or outcome you can point to?
04

what's inside

Walk me through what's included

Talk through it like you're showing a friend around. Don't worry about order.
  • List every module, template, bonus, or component
  • For each one, what does it actually help her do?
  • Anything time-sensitive, limited, or bonus-only?
05

who it's for (and who it's not)

Who should buy this, and who shouldn't?

Being specific about who this ISN'T for makes the right people trust it more.
  • What stage of business is she in?
  • What has she already tried or already know?
  • Who should skip this offer entirely?
06

the objections

What's she thinking right before she closes the tab?

Say the doubts out loud, then answer them like you would in a DM.
  • "I've tried things like this before and..."
  • "I don't have time for..."
  • "How is this different from [free thing / competitor]?"
07

the why

Why did you make this, and why now?

This is the part that makes it sound like you, not a template. Tell the real story.
  • What prompted you to create this offer?
  • Any proof it works, for you or for someone else?
  • Is there urgency: a deadline, live testing window, limited spots, launch pricing?
